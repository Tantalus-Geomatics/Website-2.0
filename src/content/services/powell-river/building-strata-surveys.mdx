A building strata typically defines the boundaries of its strata lots by reference to the physical floors, walls, and ceilings of the structure. A critical element of the building strata process is ensuring that the parent parcel is accurately defined before the strata is registered. Rule 7-2 of the **Survey and Plan Rules** mandates that prior to registration, the parent parcel must be fully monumented, any boundary discrepancies must be resolved, and natural boundaries must be established based on their present location under the **Land Title Act**. Additionally, under Section 244(1)(f) of the **Strata Property Act**, we must assess the building perimeter prior to registering the strata plan to certify that all buildings are wholly within the external boundaries of the parent parcel, or that appropriate and necessary easements exist to cover any structural encroachments.
