In <span className="text-brand-green font-semibold">Lillooet</span>, developing land near sensitive ecosystems, managing natural watercourses, or remediating historically contaminated sites requires precise spatial data and strict adherence to overlapping provincial environmental legislation. Whether you are working alongside Qualified Environmental Professionals (QEPs) to map critical fish habitat, or delineating the exact spatial limits of a contaminated site for a Site Disclosure Statement, absolute spatial certainty is primary. Under the **Land Surveyors Act**, determining the location of any natural or artificial feature—such as the top of a ravine bank, the limits of a Streamside Protection and Enhancement Area (SPEA), or the boundaries of a remediation zone—relative to a legal property boundary for the purpose of certifying it in writing is exclusively defined as the "practice of land surveying". Relying on unlicensed technicians to map these environmental constraints exposes your project to significant risks, including environmental damage, massive financial penalties, and halted development approvals.

As a dual-registered firm of British Columbia Land Surveyors (*BCLS*) and Professional Engineers, our professional geomatics team delivers the high statutory authority required to integrate your environmental obligations with your legal property boundaries. We navigate the requirements of the *Riparian Areas Protection Act* and the *Riparian Areas Protection Regulation*, ensuring that riparian assessment areas and SPEA setbacks are accurately marked to protect the natural features, functions, and conditions that support protected fish populations. Furthermore, for industrial or commercial sites, we support your compliance with the **Environmental Management Act** by precisely locating boundaries for preliminary site investigations, detailed site investigations, and remediation plans, which are subsequently recorded in the provincial Site Registry. When your project involves changes in and about a stream, our surveys provide the surveys required for authorizations under the **Water Sustainability Act**.

To capture highly complex and sensitive environmental data without disturbing the ecosystem, our firm deploys a state-of-the-art arsenal of geomatics equipment. During the preliminary site assessment in <span className="text-brand-green font-semibold">Lillooet</span>, we utilize Unmanned Aerial Systems (UAS) equipped with Aerial Drone Photogrammetry and Aerial Drone LiDAR to safely penetrate dense riparian canopies and map expansive wetlands, generating accurate 3D topographic terrain models. We establish a highly robust primary survey control network using Static GNSS and RTK GNSS Receivers, integrating the site seamlessly into the provincial active control system. In dense coastal forests or deep river ravines where satellite signals are obstructed, our survey crews deploy high-precision Robotic Total Stations to locate the precise top of bank and upland vegetation limits. To drastically enhance data collection on inaccessible terrain or highly sensitive habitats, we leverage Terrestrial LiDAR (3D laser scanning) to capture high-density point clouds, guaranteeing a perfect digital twin of the environment.
