Phased strata developments provide developers with the flexibility of developing a single unified strata community in successive stages over time. Governed by Part 13 of the **Strata Property Act**, a phased strata develops over time as a series of interim subdivisions; whereby, upon the deposit of the first phase, the strata corporation is formed, and title to the strata lots in Phase 1 are created while the remainder of the parent parcel remains a fee simple titled parcel under the **Land Title Act**. As the development progresses, subsequent phases are subdivided and registered, and the titles to the new strata lots within that phase are created.

Phased strata developments include several additional complexities beyond those required to register a traditional strata plan. These include preparations of forms P and Q for phase scheduling, consideration of phase boundary setback requirements, access temporary and permanent access requirements to the remainder parcel, and preparation of charge plans to support utility servicing to later phases.
