Before a single meter of earth is moved or a foundation is poured in <span className="text-brand-green font-semibold">District of North Vancouver</span>, accurately positioning your new improvement is a necessity. Construction layouts and the establishment of site benchmarks form the critical infrastructure that drives the success of your entire development, yet they are widely recognized as the single greatest source of liability in the land surveying profession. In British Columbia, determining the location of any natural or artificial feature, such as an excavation limit, foundation, or gridline, relative to a legal property boundary for the purpose of certifying it in writing is exclusively defined as the "practice of land surveying" under the **Land Surveyors Act**. Relying on unlicensed contractors to lay out your multi-million dollar excavation exposes your project to significant risks, including boundary encroachments, failed municipal inspections, and costly structural redesigns.
