### Project Scope

* Subdivision of a single RS-1 zoned parcel into two fee-simple lots in the District of Squamish
* Registration of a Section 219 restrictive covenant identifying the approved on-site septic field envelope for each new lot
* Building strata survey and legal strata plan filing with the Land Title Office to create a two-lot bare land strata corporation
* Topographic survey of both new lots to support architectural design for the proposed duplex structures

### Deliverables

* **Subdivision Plan:** A registered Reference Plan filed with the LTSA re-establishing and posting the new lot boundaries and access easement corridor.
* **Covenant Plan:** A statutory Section 219 covenant plan filed on title restricting future development within the designated septic reserve area.
* **Strata Plan:** A certified Building Strata Plan filed at the Land Title Office creating two strata lots, common property, and the strata corporation.
* **Topographic CAD Files:** DWG/DXF files mapping ground elevations, utility tie-ins, and setback lines for both new lots.

### Body

This project involved a complex multi-stage legal survey engagement for a private residential client in Squamish's Garibaldi Highlands neighbourhood. The client acquired a large RS-1 zoned parcel with the intent to subdivide it into two fee-simple lots, register a covenant identifying the septic reserve area, and subsequently create a two-lot bare land strata corporation to facilitate individual financing of each duplex unit.

[[image: tantalus-hero-banner.webp | float: right | width: 40 | caption: "Property corner monument set during the boundary survey"]]

Tantalus Geomatics managed the complete legal survey workflow from initial LTSA research through to final Land Title Office filings. The field crew used RTK GNSS receivers to re-establish the parent parcel boundary and confirm horizontal accuracy to within centimetre precision before setting the new corner posts dividing the two lots. The dense coastal forest cover on the eastern edge of the property required several additional site visits to maintain satellite lock during data collection.

The project required close coordination with the District of Squamish Development Services, the Ministry of Health (for the on-site septic covenant), and the client's conveyancing lawyer to sequence the three separate legal plan registrations correctly and within the municipality's subdivision approval timelines.

[[map: 49.7016,-123.1558 | zoom: 15 | height: 340]]

The subject property sits within the Garibaldi Highlands area of Squamish, close to local trail networks and within the District's standard residential subdivision permitting boundary. Coordination with the District's GIS mapping resources confirmed no conflicting easements or rights-of-way affected the proposed lot line.

Once the boundary was confirmed and the covenant registered, our team moved to the strata plan preparation phase, including a full topographic survey to support the client's architect in finalizing the duplex building envelope on each new lot.
