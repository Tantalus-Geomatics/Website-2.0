### **Body**

Translating highly complex architectural and engineering designs into physical reality in <span className="text-brand-green font-semibold">{{LOCATION_NAME}}</span> requires accurately locating the designs on the ground. Gridline layout surveys provide the fundamental geometric framework for your entire construction project, defining the placement of foundations, columns, and structural shear walls. Because gridlines are inherently tied to your property boundaries, any layout must have mm-level precision and ensure compliance with municipal setbacks, establishing this framework is an intensely regulated legal process. Under the **Land Surveyors Act**, determining the location of any artificial feature relative to a property boundary for the purpose of certifying it in writing is exclusively defined as the "practice of land surveying". Relying on unlicensed contractors to map your structural gridlines exposes your development to significant risks, including boundary encroachments and halted construction.

Tantalus Geomatics Land Surveying delivers the professional oversight necessary to conduct your gridline layout. During the layout process, we double check all critical reference points your contractor will use to re-establish building corners and structural lines to guarantee accuracy. We also establish redundant horizontal and vertical control points, including the extension of existing lines and reference targets on stable walls, ensuring your project has a reliable framework even when active construction destroys temporary survey markers.

### **Steps**

1. **Research and Pre-Calculation** The *BCLS* acquires the complete set of architectural and engineering drawings explicitly marked "Issued for Construction" or "Approved for Construction" in advance of our field deployment. By securing the CAD drawings early, our experts pre-compute the layout to identify plan misclosures or dimensional discrepancies, resolving them with your design team before construction begins. Additionally, we conduct research through the Land Title and Survey Authority of British Columbia (LTSA) to obtain the necessary plans and documents that define the parent parcel boundaries and legal encumbrances that affect the site.

2. **Field Work** Upon arriving at your site, the survey crew first locates and confirms that sufficient legal survey monuments are in place. Conducting a boundary-sensitive construction layout near unposted property boundaries incurs massive liabilities; therefore, we recommend that missing property corners be formally posted prior to construction layout or establishing the gridlines. The scope of the field work and the time required to complete the survey are directly impacted by the density of construction activity and existing infrastructure. Utilizing Robotic Total Stations, we precisely establish your structural gridlines, offset stakes, and reference points.

3. **Plan Preparation** Our technologists translate the dense 3D field data into a Sketch of Layout for Construction. This vital non-statutory document explicitly details the points set, their offsets, and, if applicable, their vertical elevations. This document serves as the authoritative document for your design team to ensure that the gridlines conform accurately to both your approved design and your legal property boundaries.

### **Deliverables**

* **Certified PDF Plans:** A digitally signed and sealed *BCLS* Sketch of Layout for Construction, explicitly detailing the gridline points set, their offsets, and elevations.

### **FAQs**

**1\. Why do I need a *British Columbia Land Surveyor* (*BCLS*) to lay out my gridlines?** Under the **Land Surveyors Act**, determining the location of any artificial feature, such as a building gridline or structural foundation, relative to a legal property boundary for the purpose of certifying it in writing is exclusively defined as the "practice of land surveying". Only a commissioned *BCLS* holds the statutory authority and liability insurance to issue this critical certification.

**2\. Why do you need the "Issued for Construction" drawings before you come to the site?** Performing complex layout calculations in the field is a recipe for serious mistakes. We require your "Issued for Construction" CAD drawings well in advance so our digital experts can pre-compute the entire layout in a controlled environment, identify any architectural misclosures, and guarantee our field placement is accurate.

**3\. What happens if the legal property pins are missing before the gridline layout begins?** Conducting boundary-sensitive construction layouts on un-posted boundaries carries immense financial and legal liability. Professional standards dictate that we must first formally post the subject boundaries and file a Posting Plan or Reference Plan in the Land Title Office prior to commencing the layout work.

**4\. Why do you use a 90-degree grid layout instead of radial measurements?** Establishing a rigid grid utilizing 90-degree angles is vastly superior to radial layouts for high-precision construction. The higher the level of accuracy required for your structural steel or concrete, the more important the grid method becomes, as it allows our surveyors and your contractors to easily double-check diagonals and adjust points on-site.

**5\. How do you ensure the gridline stakes are not placed incorrectly?** We employ a systematic quality control framework. We double-check all reference points from two independent locations or measure them in two different units to compare results. Additionally, we establish redundant horizontal and vertical control points throughout the site so we can verify positions continuously.
