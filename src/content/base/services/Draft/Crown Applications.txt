# Title

Crown Applications and Surveys

### **Body**

Unlocking the development potential of land, securing natural resources, or rectifying historical boundary issues in {{LOCATION_NAME}} often requires intricate dealings with the provincial government. Crown applications encompass a highly specialized suite of statutory procedures governed by the *Land Act* and the *Land Title Act*, requiring formal adjudication and approval by the Surveyor General of British Columbia. Whether you are applying for an exemption under Section 108 of the *Land Title Act* to prevent submerged land from reverting to the Crown, seeking to acquire closed public roads or dried watercourses under Section 58 of the *Land Act*, or establishing a public road allowance over Crown land under Section 80 of the *Land Act*, the application process demands uncompromising statutory authority and elite geomatics engineering.

In British Columbia, defining, locating, and certifying the boundaries of Crown land—and making the complex statutory applications that alter them—constitutes the "practice of land surveying" under the *Land Surveyors Act*. Only a commissioned member of the Association of British Columbia Land Surveyors (ABCLS) holds the exclusive statutory right to determine these legal boundaries and certify the official plans required by the Surveyor General. Our dual-registered team of Professional Engineers and Land Surveyors meticulously navigates the complex administrative procedures outlined in the Surveyor General's *Directions to Land Surveyors*, ensuring your applications are flawlessly prepared, legally sound, and compliant with all provincial mandates.

To meet the uncompromising precision required by the Surveyor General and the ABCLS *Survey and Plan Rules*, our firm deploys an elite arsenal of cutting-edge geomatics equipment. During the preliminary mapping of remote or heavily vegetated Crown lands in {{LOCATION\_NAME}}, we utilize Aerial Drone Photogrammetry and Aerial Drone LiDAR to generate flawless 3D topographic terrain models. We establish a robust primary survey control network by occupying points with Static GNSS and Real-Time Kinematic (RTK) GNSS Receivers, integrating the site seamlessly into the provincial active control system. In dense urban corridors, deep river ravines, or under heavy tree canopy where satellite signals are obstructed, our crews deploy millimeter-accurate Robotic Total Stations to run redundant, closed-loop traverses. For projects involving complex existing infrastructure or intricate natural boundaries, Terrestrial LiDAR (3D laser scanning) is utilized to capture a high-density 3D point cloud of the physical reality, guaranteeing your Crown application is based on absolute spatial certainty.

### **Steps**

1. **Research** The foundation of a successful Crown application is exhaustive documentary research. Acting as an impartial forensic investigator, the BCLS delves into records held by the Land Title and Survey Authority of British Columbia (LTSA), accessing the Surveyor General's Vault, original Crown grants, Crown grant tracings, historical field notes, and active title records via the secure myLTSA portal and ParcelMap BC. For most surveys over Crown land under the *Land Title Act* or the *Land Act* (with the exception of statutory right of way plans), the BCLS must formally apply to the Surveyor General for specific survey instructions and a unique parcel designation before mobilizing to the field. This critical step ensures that the government's land use plans and any underlying Indigenous interests or overlapping tenures are fully accommodated.

2. **Field Work** Once survey instructions are issued, our crews conduct a meticulous physical search to recover original survey monuments and capture the required boundary evidence. The ruggedness of the terrain, the density of the vegetation, and the remoteness of the site directly impact the scope of field work and the time required to complete the survey in {{LOCATION\_NAME}}. Utilizing RTK GNSS Receivers and Robotic Total Stations, we capture the physical evidence of natural boundaries, historical roadways, or existing improvements. The BCLS applies the common law hierarchy of evidence to adjudicate the boundaries, placing durable standard iron posts or other approved monuments in the ground to permanently mark the limits of the Crown lands being surveyed or applied for.

3. **Plan Preparation** Our digital drafting experts translate the precise 3D field data into a highly regulated statutory plan. Depending on the application, this may be a Reference Plan, Subdivision Plan, or a specific Crown Land Registry (CLRS) plan. As your certifying professional, the BCLS electronically signs the Survey Plan Certification (SPC) using a secure digital certificate. The complete application package—which may include the SPC, an Application to Deposit Plan at Land Title Office (DSPL) form, supporting evidence, and required checklists—is assembled in SurveyHub and submitted to the Surveyor General via the LTSA's Electronic Filing System (EFS) and Web Filing portals for rigorous examination and statutory approval.

### **FAQs**

**1\. Why do I need a British Columbia Land Surveyor (BCLS) for a Crown application?** Under the *Land Surveyors Act*, determining, locating, and establishing boundaries to create a legal interest in land, or certifying the location of features relative to a boundary, is exclusively defined as the "practice of land surveying". Only a commissioned BCLS holds the statutory authority to prepare the legal plans and formal applications required by the Surveyor General and the LTSA.

**2\. What is a Section 58 Land Act application?** If doubt exists as to whether land was included in a historical Crown grant, or if a watercourse or road shown in a color other than red on the original Crown grant tracing no longer exists or is no longer needed by the Province, a Section 58 application allows the Surveyor General to declare the government's intention and potentially transfer the land from the Crown to the adjacent private owner.

**3\. Do you need permission from the government before surveying Crown land?** Yes. Under the Surveyor General's Direction to Land Surveyors No. 16, prior to undertaking any survey of Crown land pursuant to the *Land Title Act* or the *Land Act* (with the exception of statutory right of way plans), a BCLS must submit an application to the Surveyor General for specific survey instructions and a unique parcel designation.

**4\. How are natural boundary adjustments handled if my waterfront property has changed?** If the shoreline of your property has changed due to lawful accretion (the gradual accumulation of new land) or if the historical plan incorrectly defined the natural boundary, the BCLS must prepare an updated survey plan and submit a formal application to the Surveyor General under Section 94 or Section 118 of the *Land Title Act* to legally update your title.

**5\. How are Crown applications and plans submitted to the government?** Crown applications and their associated survey plans are submitted electronically to the Surveyor General through the LTSA's Electronic Filing System (EFS) and Web Filing portals. Our professionals utilize platforms like SurveyHub to assemble the plan datasets, the Survey Plan Certification (SPC), and the necessary application forms (such as the DSPL or CLRS forms) to ensure seamless processing.
