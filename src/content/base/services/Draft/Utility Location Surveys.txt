### **FAQs**

**1\. What is a "ground disturbance" and why is it legally important?** A ground disturbance is defined as any work, operation, or activity that results in a disturbance of the earth regardless of depth. It is critical because striking a buried facility—such as an oil pipeline or high-voltage cable—can result in severe safety hazards to the public, massive repair costs, and immense legal liabilities,.

**2\. What do the different paint colours on the ground mean?** Field markings for underground utilities follow a strict industry colour standard: Red indicates electrical systems; Yellow indicates liquid and petroleum gas systems; Orange is for telecom systems; Blue represents water systems; and Green identifies wastewater systems.

**3\. What are the different Quality Levels (QL) for utility location?** There are four recognized levels of quality for underground utilities: QL-D relies solely on existing records or verbal recollections; QL-C involves surveying visible above-ground facilities like valves or manholes; QL-B uses geophysical equipment to determine the subsurface horizontal location and depth; and QL-A is the highest accuracy, involving physically exposing the utilities by hand or hydro-vacuuming (daylighting),,.

**4\. Who is responsible for finding utility records before a survey?** While a land surveyor conducts extensive research through the Land Title Office for registered statutory rights of way, a BC 1 Call request must be completed to supplement utility information. It is important to note that some utilities, like Shaw Cable and Pacific Northern Gas (PNG), are not part of the BC 1 Call registry and must be contacted directly.

**5\. Why do I need a British Columbia Land Surveyor (BCLS) to map my utilities?** Under the *Land Surveyors Act*, determining the location of any artificial feature, such as a buried utility or conduit, relative to a legal property boundary for the purpose of certifying it in writing is exclusively defined as the "practice of land surveying",. A BCLS provides the statutory authority and professional liability insurance required to integrate your utility mapping with your property lines safely.
