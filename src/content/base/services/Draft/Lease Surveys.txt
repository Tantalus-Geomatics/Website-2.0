# Title

Lease Surveys

### **Body**

Developing, occupying, or extracting resources from land in {{LOCATION\_NAME}} often requires the strategic use of leases to secure long-term legal rights without the need to transfer fee-simple ownership. Whether you are leasing a commercial building footprint, severing a portion of a private parcel for development, or securing a major resource tenure on provincial Crown land, defining the exact spatial extent of a lease is an intensely regulated legal process. As a dual-registered firm of British Columbia Land Surveyors (BCLS) and Professional Engineers, our digital and technical experts deliver the elite geomatics engineering and uncompromising statutory authority required to flawlessly map your leasehold interests and secure your investments.

In British Columbia, the leasing of privately owned, fee-simple land is strictly governed by the *Land Title Act*. Under Section 73(1) of the Act, a person must not subdivide land into smaller parcels for the purpose of leasing it, or agreeing to lease it, for a term exceeding three years except in strict compliance with the subdivision requirements of the Act. Attempting to partition land for a long-term lease without a certified survey plan exposes developers and property owners to severe legal liabilities and title defects. However, Section 73(3) of the Act provides a critical statutory exception: the stringent subdivision requirements do not apply to a subdivision for the purpose of leasing a building or part of a building. Furthermore, under Section 5 of the *Property Law Act*, a landlord who creates a lease for a term exceeding three years is legally obligated to deliver an instrument to the tenant in a form that is registrable under the *Land Title Act*.

Conversely, securing leasehold rights over public land requires navigating the *Land Act*. Under Section 11 and Section 38 of the *Land Act*, the Minister has the authority to dispose of Crown land by issuing a lease subject to specific terms and reservations that the Minister considers advisable in the public interest. The legislation imposes strict limits on the scale of these tenures; Crown land of an area greater than 520 hectares must not be disposed of by lease to any one applicant, although the Minister may grant exceptions for specific uses such as grazing, commercial, industrial, railway, airport, or watershed purposes. Additionally, a Crown land lease must not be issued for a term longer than 60 years without the prior approval of the Minister.

To define these highly specific lease areas with the absolute precision required by the Surveyor General and the Land Title and Survey Authority (LTSA), our firm deploys an elite arsenal of cutting-edge geomatics equipment. For expansive Crown land leases, we utilize Aerial Drone Photogrammetry and Aerial Drone LiDAR to penetrate dense canopies and generate flawless 3D topographic terrain models of the wilderness. We establish a highly robust primary geodetic control network using Static GNSS and RTK GNSS measurements to seamlessly integrate your site into the provincial active control system. In dense urban corridors in {{LOCATION\_NAME}} or when defining complex indoor lease areas for commercial buildings, we deploy millimeter-accurate Robotic Total Stations and Terrestrial LiDAR (3D laser scanning) to capture high-density point clouds of the as-built conditions, ensuring that your lease boundaries flawlessly reflect the physical reality of the structure.

### **Steps**

1. **Research** The foundation of a legally unassailable lease survey is exhaustive documentary research. Acting in our capacity as an impartial professional forensic investigator, the BCLS accesses the Land Title and Survey Authority of British Columbia (LTSA) via the secure myLTSA portal to retrieve current Certificates of Title, historical subdivision plans, original Crown grants, and surveyor field notes. We meticulously review all registered encumbrances, such as existing rights of way and restrictive covenants, to ensure your proposed lease area is free from spatial conflicts and perfectly accommodates existing legal constraints.  
2. **Field Work** To flawlessly map the lease boundaries, our survey crews conduct a rigorous physical search to recover original survey monuments and capture the physical features of the site. The ruggedness of the terrain, density of the vegetation, and the stage of construction directly impact the scope of field work and the time required to complete the survey. Utilizing our RTK GNSS and Robotic Total Stations, we capture physical evidence and place new standard iron posts to define surface land leases. For building leases, our crews utilize Terrestrial LiDAR to meticulously map the interior structural walls, balancing out minor construction discrepancies to establish unassailable spatial limits.  
3. **Plan Preparation** Our digital drafting experts translate the precise 3D field data into a highly regulated statutory plan. Depending on the type of lease, this will typically be a Reference Plan or an Explanatory Plan. For example, an Explanatory plan for the lease of part of a building must explicitly indicate in the plan title how many stories the building comprises, and the body of the plan must indicate which floor the lease area is situated on. As your certifying professional, the BCLS electronically signs the Survey Plan Certification using a secure digital certificate, taking full statutory responsibility for the plan before submitting the complete package to the LTSA or the Surveyor General for official deposit.

### **FAQs**

**1\. Can I lease a portion of my land for 10 years without doing a subdivision?** No. Under Section 73(1) of the *Land Title Act*, leasing a portion of an unsubdivided parcel of land for a term exceeding three years is legally considered a subdivision. You must engage a BCLS to prepare a formal subdivision or reference plan, which must be approved by an Approving Officer, before the lease can be legally registered.

**2\. Does the three-year lease restriction apply to leasing a space inside a commercial building?** No. Section 73(3) of the *Land Title Act* provides a specific exemption stating that the restrictions on subdividing land for lease do not apply to a subdivision for the purpose of leasing a building or part of a building. Our surveyors can prepare an Explanatory Plan of the building footprint or interior without requiring municipal subdivision approval.

**3\. What are the maximum size and term limits for a lease on Crown land?** Under the *Land Act*, a Crown land lease cannot exceed an area of 520 hectares to any one applicant, nor can it be issued for a term longer than 60 years, unless the Minister grants specific prior approval for exceptional purposes like commercial, industrial, railway, or airport development.

**4\. What happens if I sign a long-term lease for a portion of land but do not register a survey plan?** While Section 73.1 of the *Land Title Act* states that an unregistered lease of a part of a parcel is not completely unenforceable between the original parties merely because it doesn't comply with subdivision rules, failing to register the lease creates massive risks. Without a registered plan, your lease is not protected by the Torrens system, meaning future purchasers of the land may take title free and clear of your unregistered leasehold interest.

**5\. Why do I need a British Columbia Land Surveyor (BCLS) for my lease plan?** Under the *Land Surveyors Act*, determining, defining, and establishing boundaries to create a legal interest in land is exclusively defined as the "practice of land surveying". Only a commissioned BCLS holds the statutory authority, professional liability insurance, and technical expertise required to prepare and certify the legal plans required by the Land Title Office or the Surveyor General.
